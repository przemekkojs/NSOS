## Understanding Your Hour Register

Your Personal Hour Register shows all your teaching assignments for the active semester.

### Reading Your Register

Each teaching group entry shows:

```
Subject: Introduction to Databases (CS-101)
Group: Group 1A
Type: Lecture
Hours/Week: 2
Total Weeks: 15
Total Hours: 30
Schedule: Monday 10:00-12:00
Room: Building A, Room 301
```

**Key fields explained:**

- **Subject** - Course name and code
- **Group** - Specific section or class identifier
- **Type** - Lecture, Lab, Tutorial, etc.
- **Hours/Week** - Weekly teaching hours for this group
- **Total Weeks** - Number of weeks in semester
- **Total Hours** - Hours/Week √ó Total Weeks = Total semester hours
- **Schedule** - Day and time of class
- **Room** - Location where class meets

### Hour Calculations

At the bottom of your register, you'll see summary calculations:

```
Total Assigned Hours: 185
Your Pensum: 180
Status: Overtime +5 hours
```

**Understanding the calculations:**

| Scenario         | Example                    | Status                   |
| ---------------- | -------------------------- | ------------------------ |
| **Under Pensum** | Assigned: 160, Pensum: 180 | Remaining: 20 hours (üü¢) |
| **At Pensum**    | Assigned: 180, Pensum: 180 | Full: 0 hours (üü°)       |
| **Overtime**     | Assigned: 195, Pensum: 180 | Overtime: +15 hours (üî¥) |

::: info How Hours Are Calculated
Your total assigned hours = sum of all (Hours/Week √ó Weeks) for each teaching group. This is compared against your pensum to determine your status.
:::

## Common Teacher Scenarios

### Scenario 1: You're Under Pensum

**What it means:** You're assigned fewer hours than your contract requires.

**What to expect:**

- You may receive additional assignments during the semester
- This is normal early in the semester during planning
- You're available for new teaching groups if needed

**What to do:**

- Monitor your register for new assignments
- Be prepared for possible additions
- Contact administrator if you want more hours

### Scenario 2: You're Near Pensum

**What it means:** You're close to your contracted hours (95-100%).

**What to expect:**

- Your schedule is likely finalized
- Small adjustments still possible
- You're optimally utilized

**What to do:**

- Review your schedule carefully
- Confirm all details are correct
- Report any issues immediately (limited flexibility)

### Scenario 3: You're in Overtime

**What it means:** You're assigned more hours than your contract specifies.

**What to expect:**

- Administrator is aware of the situation
- May be temporary during planning phase
- Could result in additional compensation (check institutional policy)
- Assignments may be redistributed

**What to do:**

- Verify the hours are correct
- Check with administrator about the plan
- Understand institutional overtime policy
- Be patient as workload is rebalanced

### Scenario 4: You Notice an Error

**What it means:** Something in your register doesn't look right.

**Common errors:**

- Wrong subject or group assigned
- Incorrect hours/week value
- Duplicate assignments
- Missing teaching groups
- Wrong room or schedule

**What to do:**

1. Document the error specifically
2. Contact your administrator immediately
3. Provide correct information
4. Confirm when correction is made
5. Check your register after update

### Scenario 5: Schedule Changes During Semester

**What it means:** Your assignments are modified mid-semester.

**Possible reasons:**

- Enrollment changes require section adjustments
- Another teacher's leave requires coverage
- Room conflicts need resolution
- Administrative error correction

**What to do:**

- Check your register when notified of changes
- Verify the new assignment details
- Note impact on total hours
- Adjust your planning accordingly
- Confirm changes with administrator if unclear

## Understanding Your Employment

### Position Types

Your academic position determines your base pensum:

| Position                | Typical Pensum Range   | Focus                               |
| ----------------------- | ---------------------- | ----------------------------------- |
| **Professor**           | 120-150 hours/semester | Research, oversight, some teaching  |
| **Associate Professor** | 150-180 hours/semester | Balance of research and teaching    |
| **Assistant Professor** | 180-210 hours/semester | Heavy teaching, developing research |
| **Lecturer**            | 210-240 hours/semester | Primary focus on teaching           |

::: info Your Specific Pensum
Your exact pensum is determined by your position, employment type (full-time, part-time), and institutional policy. Check your contract or ask HR for details.
:::

### Employment Types

**Full-Time:**

- Standard pensum based on position
- Full benefits
- Year-round employment

**Part-Time:**

- Proportional pensum (e.g., 50% time = 50% of full pensum)
- May have reduced benefits
- Flexible arrangements

### Overtime Policies

Overtime handling varies by institution:

**Common approaches:**

- Additional hourly compensation
- Compensatory time off
- Rolled into next semester adjustments
- Optional teaching with extra pay
- No compensation (workload expected to balance)

::: warning Check Your Institution's Policy
NSOS tracks overtime hours, but compensation and policies are determined by your institution. Review your employment contract or speak with HR.
:::

## Frequently Asked Questions

### About Your Schedule

**Q: How often is my schedule updated?**
A: In real-time. Changes made by administrators appear immediately in your hour register.

**Q: Can I see next semester's schedule?**
A: Only the active semester is visible. Next semester assignments appear when that semester becomes active.

**Q: Why do some groups show 0 hours?**
A: This may be a data entry error or placeholder. Contact your administrator for clarification.

### About Hours and Overtime

**Q: I'm showing overtime. Do I get paid extra?**
A: This depends on your institution's policy. NSOS tracks the hours; compensation is handled separately by HR/payroll.

**Q: My pensum seems wrong. Can I change it?**
A: No, only administrators can modify pensum. If you believe it's incorrect, contact your administrator with your contract details.

**Q: Do office hours count toward my pensum?**
A: This depends on institutional policy. NSOS typically tracks only formal teaching hours. Check your contract for specifics.

### About the System

**Q: Why can't I modify my schedule?**
A: Teachers have read-only access to maintain data integrity and ensure proper administrative oversight of assignments.

**Q: I can't log in. What should I do?**
A: Contact your system administrator or IT support. They can reset passwords and troubleshoot access issues.

**Q: Can I access NSOS on my phone?**
A: Yes, NSOS is web-based and works on mobile devices. Use your regular login credentials.
